# [317. Shortest Distance from All Buildings](https://leetcode.com/problems/shortest-distance-from-all-buildings/)

Step 1 — Nail the Problem Statement

  You have a grid. Some cells are buildings (1). You want to place a house on an empty cell (0) such that
  the sum of distances to every building is minimized.

  Two constraints: buildings and obstacles (2) block movement.





 Step 2 — Naive Approach (and Why It's Slow)

  Your first instinct might be:

  ▎ "For every empty cell, BFS to every building, sum the distances, take the minimum."

  That's correct but slow. If there are E empty cells and B buildings, you run E × B BFS calls. On a 50×50
   grid, that's thousands of BFS traversals.





 **What Do We Actually Want?**

  For every empty cell, we want:

  total_distance(cell) = dist_to_building_1 + dist_to_building_2 + dist_to_building_3 + ...

  Then we pick the cell with the smallest total_distance.
